How the Department of Health (DOH) Can Drive Health Information Technology (HIT) in the Philippines

Published: Thu 19 September 2024
By Jeriel De Silos, MD

In Health Informatics.

tags: health technology HIT DOH

Introduction

Health Information Technology (HIT) plays a pivotal role in modernizing healthcare delivery, and the Department of Health (DOH) is in a prime position to lead its adoption in the Philippines. By leveraging HIT, the DOH can help improve healthcare outcomes, streamline services, and promote better health information exchange. This article explores how the DOH can drive HIT adoption in the Philippines, with insights from relevant studies and policies.

1. Developing a National HIT Strategy

A comprehensive national HIT strategy aligned with the Philippines' Universal Health Care (UHC) Law is critical. The DOH has already laid the groundwork with the Philippine eHealth Strategic Framework and Plan (2014–2020), which outlines a roadmap for HIT development across the country. By updating and expanding this strategy to encompass emerging technologies like artificial intelligence and blockchain, the DOH can ensure HIT initiatives remain relevant and scalable.

2. Enhancing Interoperability Among Health Systems

Fragmented health information systems remain a challenge, often resulting in inefficient care delivery. Interoperability—where healthcare systems can seamlessly share and exchange data—is vital. The DOH can adopt international standards like HL7 and FHIR, which are commonly used to ensure compatibility between electronic health records (EHRs) across different healthcare providers. In fact, a study by Abouelmehdi et al. (2017) emphasizes that interoperability improves patient care and reduces the risk of medical errors.

3. Strengthening Health Data Governance

With the rise of digital health records, data governance becomes a critical issue. The Data Privacy Act of 2012 in the Philippines mandates the protection of sensitive personal information, which includes health data. The DOH can lead the enforcement of these policies, ensuring that healthcare institutions are compliant with the law. Additionally, frameworks like COBIT-2019 provide a structured approach to managing health information systems and data governance.

4. Facilitating Capacity Building for Health Workers

For HIT to succeed, healthcare professionals must be equipped with the necessary skills to use these systems effectively. In a survey conducted by the World Health Organization (WHO) on digital health, training and capacity-building programs were highlighted as essential for healthcare workers to adopt and utilize HIT efficiently. The DOH can collaborate with educational institutions and professional organizations to provide continuous education on topics such as EMR systems, telemedicine, and mobile health (mHealth).

5. Expanding Telemedicine and Remote Health Services

Telemedicine has seen a significant rise in adoption due to the COVID-19 pandemic, particularly in underserved areas. The DOH can build on this momentum by expanding telemedicine initiatives through partnerships with the Department of Information and Communications Technology (DICT) and private healthcare providers. Studies have shown that telemedicine not only improves healthcare access but also reduces costs associated with travel and physical consultations.

6. Supporting Local Health IT Innovations

The Philippines is home to a growing number of health IT startups, and the DOH can foster innovation by collaborating with these enterprises. For instance, companies like mClinica have developed digital tools that streamline pharmaceutical supply chains and improve patient outcomes. The DOH can provide grants, technical support, and regulatory guidance to support local innovations that address the country’s specific healthcare challenges.

Conclusion

The Department of Health is poised to drive Health Information Technology in the Philippines. By developing an updated national HIT strategy, promoting interoperability, enforcing health data governance, facilitating capacity building for health workers, expanding telemedicine, and supporting local innovations, the DOH can pave the way for a more efficient and equitable healthcare system. Through these efforts, HIT can significantly contribute to the realization of the Universal Health Care law and improve the overall health outcomes for Filipinos.
